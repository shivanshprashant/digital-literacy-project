
# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

## The Do's (Professional Hygiene)
1. **Do curate a professional narrative:** Ensure your LinkedIn and GitHub reflect your current academic focus and technical milestones.
2. **Do separate personal and professional identities:** Keep platforms like Instagram private while leaving technical networking platforms public.
3. **Do verify before sharing:** Always fact-check articles or data before amplifying them on your networks to maintain your credibility.
4. **Do engage constructively:** Comment thoughtfully on industry leaders' posts or repository discussions to build a verifiable network.
5. **Do audit your digital footprint:** Regularly search your name to ensure no outdated or unprofessional content is publicly accessible to future employers.

## The Don'ts (Risk Mitigation)
1. **Don't post in the heat of the moment:** Never use public platforms to vent frustrations about professors, university administration, or peers.
2. **Don't share proprietary or academic code:** Avoid posting solutions to active assignments or confidential internship data, as this violates academic integrity and NDAs.
3. **Don't ignore privacy settings:** Never assume the default settings protect your data; actively configure who can view your activity.
4. **Don't use overly casual language in professional spaces:** Avoid using text-speak or slang when messaging recruiters or alumni on LinkedIn.
5. **Don't engage in digital arguments:** Participating in toxic online debates damages your professional reputation and leaves a permanent, searchable record.
