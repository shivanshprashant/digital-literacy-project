
# Stay Safe Online: Cybersecurity Checklist for Students

1. **Never enter your UPI PIN to receive money:** A PIN is only required to *send* funds or check your balance. [cite: 83]
2. **Verify the VPA (Virtual Payment Address):** Always double-check the receiver's name that appears on your banking app before authorizing any UPI transfer. [cite: 83]
3. **Use official channels only:** Never trust customer care numbers found on random Google searches; always use the official app or website's help section.
4. **Deny screen-sharing requests:** Never download apps like AnyDesk or TeamViewer at the instruction of an unknown caller.
5. **Enable transaction limits:** Set a strict daily limit on your UPI and debit cards to mitigate potential losses.
6. **Use a secondary account for online expenses:** Keep your primary savings separate from the account linked to your daily UPI scanner.
7. **Implement 2FA everywhere:** Ensure two-factor authentication is active on your email, banking, and social media infrastructure.
8. [cite_start]**Report immediately:** If compromised, immediately freeze your accounts and report the incident to the **National Cyber Crime Portal (cybercrime.gov.in)** or call the **Helpline 1930**. [cite: 84]
