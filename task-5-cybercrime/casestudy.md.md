
# Case Study: The Refund Social Engineering Exploit (UPI Fraud)

**The Crime:** Online Payment/UPI Fraud via Social Engineering.

**The Scenario:** A college student booked a train ticket for the upcoming holidays but had to cancel it. When the refund was delayed, the student searched for the railway platform's customer care number on a generic search engine. The top result was a fraudulent, sponsored ad containing a fake helpline number. 

**Step-by-Step Execution:**
1. The student dialed the fake number. The scammer, posing as a support executive, apologized for the delay and promised an instant refund.
2. The scammer instructed the student to download a screen-sharing application (like AnyDesk) under the guise of "verifying the device for the refund gateway."
3. The scammer then sent a UPI payment request to the student's phone, telling them to "enter your UPI PIN to authenticate the incoming refund."
4. Because the scammer was viewing the screen, they saw the student type the PIN. 

**The Target & Consequences:** College students and the elderly are prime targets due to urgency and reliance on quick digital transactions. The consequence in this scenario was the immediate unauthorized transfer of ₹15,000 from the student's linked bank account, completely depleting their monthly budget.
